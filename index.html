About Ants

Part 1: What Ants Do

  • Provide nutrients for your garden and soil health with decomposition and tilling.
  • Actually do pest control by feeding on ticks, termites, and stinkbugs.
  • Disperse seeds and create new infrastructure on yards through ant hills.
  • Help aerate soil through their complex tunnel systems.
  • Attract other life nearby for a backyard party.

    The mere presence of ants is an indicator of a healthy ecosystem.

Types of Ants

Here are my favourites:

Solenopsis Fugax

This thief ant will steal the young of the Tapinoma ants to eat. When they find a brood chamber, they will release pheromones that repel the other ants forcing them to run while they abscond the brood to be eaten alive. Their poison is enough to keep other worker ants from 18 species away for up to an hour

Formica archboldi

These ants will use chemically similar odors to infiltrate trap jaw worker ants and spray a powerful acid to paralyze them. In which they dismember and store them in their nest. They literally collect the heads of their enemies.

Formica paralugubris

AKA wood ants or carpenter ants live in dense groups in warm, humid, environments with dead insects making them target for widespread disease. To avoid epidemics they groom each other and even collect a antimicrobial tree resin back to their nest to help the injured.
